# Governance Charter

This platform is a governance decision-support system. It is not a surveillance
tool, an individual targeting system, or an automated enforcement layer.

## Operating Boundaries

- No surveillance of individuals.
- No individual targeting, watchlists, or person-level scoring.
- No predictive policing workflows.
- Aggregated, regional, and privacy-safe indicators only.
- Human review remains mandatory for operational decisions.
- Auditability is required for access, policy changes, and intervention records.

## Intended Use

- Detect emerging instability at the regional level.
- Help officials understand likely drivers of pressure.
- Support proportionate, documented, cross-agency coordination.
- Track outcomes so intervention quality can be reviewed later.

## Roles And Workflows

National workflow:

- Set policy packs, governance constraints, and operating thresholds.
- Review cross-county risk posture and monthly national trends.
- Approve model promotion, exports, and policy changes.

County workflow:

- Review county-specific risk and driver summaries.
- Coordinate field validation and non-coercive intervention options.
- Record outcomes, notes, and operational follow-up.

Baseline user roles:

- `viewer`: read posture, alerts, and approved reports.
- `analyst`: inspect drivers, explanations, and submit notes.
- `operator`: execute approved workflows and capture outcomes.
- `admin`: manage policy, users, and system configuration.

## Pilot Scope

The initial pilot should remain narrow:

- one to two counties,
- county-level aggregation,
- weekly county reviews and monthly national review,
- a limited set of approved data sources,
- explicit exclusion of person-level and raw operational targeting data.

## Success Standard

The charter is successful when stakeholders can immediately distinguish the
platform from surveillance tooling and understand that it exists to improve
restraint, auditability, and timing.
